Retinitis Pigmentosa

(scroll down)

Retinitis Pigmentosa (RP) is a degenerative eye condition that affects the retina. RP causes the cells that detect light to break down, causing vision loss.

RP is a genetic disease occurring in about 1 out of every 4,000 people in the United States.

Everyone's experience with RP is different.


The following images give an idea of what life is like for many people with RP.

This is what a scene looks like with normal vision.

Over time the photoreceptors die and the field of view narrows.

This causes tunnel vision as the peripheral vision is lost.

The brain attempts to fill in the missing information creating an out of focus view in the periphery.

The view continues to narrow over time.

For some it can narrow to a very narrow window.

Or complete blindness.

In many cases, there is an increased contrast in the surroundings making it hard to discern details in the shadows.

The narrowed vision can happen anywhere in the field of vision leading to an off-center area of focus.

There also are many types of other splotches and streaks that impede vision in the area that is still functioning.

Everyone experiences RP differently. This is just an example of some of the ways vision is affected by RP.
